Artifact Overview

Title: Evolution of Artificial Intelligence Timeline

Objective: To research and present the historical development of artificial intelligence by identifying major milestones, influential researchers, and technological breakthroughs that shaped modern AI systems.

Process: I researched academic and industry sources on the history of artificial intelligence and identified key technological milestones. These events were then organized into a chronological visual timeline to help readers understand how AI developed over time.

Tools: HTML and CSS were used to build the webpage layout. Research was conducted using academic sources and educational content. Generative AI tools such as ChatGPT assisted in organizing and summarizing information.

Value Proposition: This artifact demonstrates my ability to research emerging technologies, synthesize complex historical developments, and communicate technical ideas through structured visual storytelling.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has evolved through decades of scientific discovery, technological innovation, and industrial competition. This artifact presents a visual timeline highlighting the key breakthroughs, influential researchers, and technological developments that shaped modern AI and machine learning.

The timeline also identifies periods known as AI Winters, when progress slowed due to limitations in computing power or unrealistic expectations. It concludes with the recent surge in AI capabilities enabled by deep learning, GPUs, cloud computing, and large-scale datasets.

Key Milestones in Artificial Intelligence

1950

Turing Test

Alan Turing proposed the concept of evaluating machine intelligence through conversation. His famous question, “Can machines think?”, became a foundational philosophical challenge for AI research.

1956

Dartmouth Conference

Often considered the birth of artificial intelligence as a research field, the Dartmouth Workshop introduced the term “Artificial Intelligence.” Researchers such as John McCarthy and Marvin Minsky envisioned machines capable of reasoning and learning.

1974-1980

First AI Winter

Early optimism about AI led to unrealistic expectations. When systems failed to deliver human-level reasoning, research funding declined dramatically. Limited computing power and weak algorithms prevented progress.

1997

IBM Deep Blue

IBM’s Deep Blue defeated world chess champion Garry Kasparov. This milestone demonstrated that machines could outperform humans in complex strategic tasks.

2012

AlexNet and the Deep Learning Breakthrough

AlexNet, developed by Geoffrey Hinton’s team, dramatically improved image recognition accuracy in the ImageNet competition. The success of GPU-based neural networks triggered the modern deep learning revolution.

2016

AlphaGo

Google DeepMind’s AlphaGo defeated Go world champion Lee Sedol using reinforcement learning and neural networks. Go had long been considered too complex for machines due to its enormous number of possible board states.

2022

Generative AI and ChatGPT

Large Language Models (LLMs) such as GPT-3 and ChatGPT introduced new capabilities in language understanding, reasoning, and content generation. Generative AI rapidly expanded into fields such as education, healthcare, programming, and creative industries.

Major Technological Drivers of Modern AI

GPU Computing

Graphics Processing Units (GPUs) dramatically accelerated neural network training. Companies like NVIDIA built specialized hardware that enabled large-scale deep learning models.

Cloud Computing

Cloud platforms from Amazon, Google, and Microsoft allow researchers to train large models using distributed computing resources and massive datasets.

Big Data

Modern AI systems rely on large training datasets. The growth of the internet and digital platforms provided the data required for machine learning models to improve.

Influential Figures in AI

Alan Turing

Pioneer of theoretical computer science and creator of the Turing Test.

Geoffrey Hinton

Known as the “Godfather of Deep Learning,” Hinton played a central role in modern neural network development.

Yann LeCun

Developer of convolutional neural networks used widely in computer vision systems.
