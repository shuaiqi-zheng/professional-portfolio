Commercial AI Applications

Healthcare

Google + DocMorris Digital Health Guide

Google and DocMorris announced a partnership to create a more personalized digital health experience in Europe. The collaboration includes an AI-based health guide, conversational support in the pharmacy journey, and cloud infrastructure to support secure digital services.

Business impact: This use case helps customers move more easily from health questions to prescription and pharmacy services, while helping the business scale support and reduce friction in the customer journey.

Decision-making value: AI acts as a navigation layer that helps patients decide what to do next and helps the company route users toward the right service more efficiently.

Financial Services

Visa Intelligent Commerce Connect

Visa introduced Intelligent Commerce Connect to help businesses participate in AI-driven shopping through a single integration. The platform is designed for merchants, agent builders, and commerce enablers supporting AI-assisted purchasing.

Business impact: This use case reduces transaction friction and prepares businesses for agentic commerce, where AI systems may search, compare, and help complete purchases.

Decision-making value: AI is no longer just recommending products. It is moving closer to influencing and executing purchase decisions within secure payment flows.

Manufacturing

Siemens Fuse EDA AI Agent

Siemens launched the Fuse EDA AI Agent for semiconductor, 3D IC, and PCB workflows. The system is designed to plan and orchestrate complex engineering tasks across design, verification, and manufacturing sign-off.

Business impact: This application reduces manual coordination across highly technical workflows, shortens development cycles, and helps engineering teams work more efficiently.

Decision-making value: AI supports experts by coordinating multi-step processes and surfacing the right information faster, improving operational decisions in complex product environments.

Selected Use Case Analysis

Use case focus: Visa Intelligent Commerce Connect. This is especially significant because it shows AI moving from support into execution. In many earlier AI applications, the system helped users search or compare options. Visa’s platform suggests a near future in which AI agents can also help complete the commercial transaction itself. That matters because it changes how businesses may design digital storefronts, optimize product information, and think about customer journeys. If AI agents become regular participants in online shopping, businesses will need to serve both human buyers and machine-assisted buyers.

Conclusion

These three examples show that commercial AI is already creating measurable value across industries. In healthcare, AI improves digital guidance and patient support. In financial services, it is reshaping commerce and payment flows. In manufacturing, it is accelerating highly technical workflows. The larger lesson is that AI adoption is becoming most valuable when it is embedded directly into important business processes rather than treated as a separate experimental tool.
